PHASE 6 — BOARD DEMO REHEARSAL

No new features.

No localization.

No refactoring.

No architecture changes.

Your job is to simulate a live board presentation and identify anything that would make a board member question the product.

==================================================
SCENARIO
========

Assume:

School: Foundation School Humhama
Location: Srinagar, Kashmir, India

Audience:

* Principal
* Vice Principal
* School Board
* Finance Representative

They are seeing the product for the first time.

==================================================
DEMO WALKTHROUGH
================

Walk through exactly this sequence:

1. Login
2. Admin Dashboard
3. Students Directory
4. Student 360 Profile
5. Attendance
6. Academics
7. Fees
8. Transport
9. Announcements
10. Messages
11. AI Insights
12. Meetings
13. VP Dashboard
14. Teacher Dashboard
15. Parent Dashboard
16. Student Dashboard
17. Settings

For every screen answer:

A. What is the strongest thing a board member sees?

B. What question would a skeptical board member ask?

C. Can the UI answer that question immediately?

D. Does any number look fake?

E. Does any data look stale?

F. Does anything contradict another screen?

==================================================
RED TEAM REVIEW
===============

Act like the most skeptical board member.

Identify:

* statistics that look invented
* counts that look suspicious
* dates that look stale
* rankings that look hardcoded
* KPIs without explanation
* empty states
* placeholder content
* fake-looking AI outputs
* unrealistic school metrics

==================================================
SCORING
=======

Rate:

Credibility
Data realism
Operational realism
Board confidence
Demo readiness

Score each 1–10.

==================================================
OUTPUT
======

Provide:

1. Screen-by-screen board reaction
2. Tough questions the board may ask
3. Screens most likely to cause concern
4. Top 10 remaining demo risks
5. Final recommendation:

   * Ready
   * Ready with caveats
   * Not ready

Do not write code.

Do not fix anything.

Only evaluate the demo experience.
